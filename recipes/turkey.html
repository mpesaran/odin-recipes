Chicken Pasta

Description

Cooking turkey in a bag is easy with this recipe. Your Thanksgiving turkey will be perfectly moist when done, and you can make gravy out of the juice that forms in the bottom of the bag. Plus, cleanup is a snap! The cooking time will vary if your turkey is larger or smaller than 12 pounds.

Ingredients

  • Turkey
  • Flour
  • Celery
  • Onions
  • Salt & Pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Remove and discard giblets.Rinse turkey and pat dry. Sprinkle the bottom of the turkey bag with flour.
  3. Place turkey in the bag and add celery and onions.
  4. Seal the bag and transfer into a roasting pan; poke several holes in the bag with a fork.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven until turkey is cooked and the juices run clear, about 3 to 3 1/2 hours. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the thigh meat should read 180 degrees F (85 degrees C).
